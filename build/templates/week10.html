Cowboys Gameday: Week 10

Cowboys at Steelers

3:25 p.m. Sunday TV: Ch. 4

Radio: KRLD-FM (105.3), KMVK-FM (107.5, Spanish)

Line: Steelers by 2½

    A win would mean ...

    The Cowboys win eight in a row, tying the 1977 team for the longest win streak in franchise history. Speaking of streaks, Dallas would also improve its current run against the AFC North to 6-0 and win its sixth of the last eight meetings against the pedigree Steelers.

    A loss would mean ...

    Quarterback Dak Prescott and Dallas have to deal with a defeat for the first time since way back in the Sept. 11 season opener, more than two months ago. The Steelers would tie the series at 16-16 in the Cowboys’ first road defeat of the season.

    Brandon George: Sure, Pittsburgh presents a daunting road challenge for the Cowboys. The Steelers are one of the league’s top home teams. But this Cowboys team is on a roll and is no slouch on the road. The Cowboys have won 15 of their last 20 regular-season road games. They have already won at Lambeau Field this year against the Packers. They will conquer Heinz Field on Sunday to win their eighth consecutive game and improve to 5-0 on the road. The Steelers just got back quarterback Ben Roethlisberger and have been sputtering, losing their last three games. They won’t turn it around against a Cowboys offense that has dominated time of possession. Cowboys, 27-24

    Kate Hairopoulos: The Steelers are 3-1 this season at Heinz Field — a tricky place for Dallas to match the franchise’s longest winning streak at eight games. But that could make pulling it off all the sweeter. If the Cowboys are to do it, they’ll need rookie QB Dak Prescott to take care of the ball. That’s been a known quality of his for the most part, considering he didn’t commit a single turnover in his first four games. But before the Cowboys destroyed the lowly Cleveland Browns last week, Prescott had committed four turnovers (two interceptions, two fumbles) in three games, including three in the red zone. The Steelers have four interceptions and five fumble recoveries this season. Cowboys, 28-24

    Jon Machota: The Steelers have been great at home for a long time. They’ve won 19 of their last 25 at Heinz Field. This will be the Cowboys’ toughest challenge since facing Aaron Rodgers at Lambeau Field. They won 30-16, making Rodgers look average. Will they do the same to future Hall of Famer Ben Roethlisberger? Big Ben had left knee surgery a month ago and struggled in his first game back last week. But he’ll be better at home. In his three home starts this season, Roethlisberger has thrown 12 TDs to only two INTs. For his career, Roethlisberger is 68-21 at home. Give me the two-time Super Bowl champ to pull it out in the fourth quarter. Steelers, 27-24

    David Moore: The Cowboys are the only team that hasn’t allowed a 100-yard rusher or receiver this season. That will change Sunday. Pittsburgh’s Antonio Brown is in the conversation when it comes to the NFL’s top receiver. Le’Veon Bell is a threat at running back. He rushed for 144 yards on only 18 carries against Kansas City in October. One or the other — and worst-case scenario, both — should end the Cowboys’ 100-yard shutout streak. Brown is the most likely candidate. But don’t discount Bell. It’s surprising no team has pounded away at this defense on the ground as a way to counter the Cowboys’ dominance in time of possession. Bell could be the first. Steelers, 27-24

    Tim Cowlishaw: Dallas surrenders highest point total of the season, but offense refuses to slow its roll and the club matches its longest single-season win streak ever. Cowboys, 27-26

    Rick Gosselin: You can get by without half your starting secondary against Cleveland but not against Ben Roethlisberger, Antonio Brown and the Steelers. Steelers, 31-27

    Barry Horn: You can't win 'em all. A Cowboys tight end drops what looked to be a sure TD pass in the end zone. Roger Staubach shakes his head. Steelers, 27-24

    Kevin Sherrington: Garrett tells Romo if he asks to go in one more time, he's not taking him on the next road trip. Cowboys, 27-21

    Bob Sturm’s scouting report

    SituationEdgeIn the know

    When the Cowboys run the ball

    Edge

    At the risk of being repetitious, the Cowboys are going to receive the advantage in this category every week until someone shows the ability to slow Ezekiel Elliott down. Pittsburgh is formidable against the run, but the Cowboys are used to wearing down their opponent with stubbornness in play-calling. The Cowboys know that while they have been slowed on the ground, nobody has stopped them.

    When the Cowboys pass the ball

    Edge

    The most unlikely statistic that exists in the NFL these days has the Steelers tied for last in the league in sacks. “Blitzburgh” is trying to figure out how to rattle quarterbacks without exposing their very young secondary and are having a hard time getting consistent results. This could be a real challenge for Dak Prescott and Dez Bryant, but look for plenty of Cole Beasley underneath and Elliott on swing passes.

    When the Steelers run the ball

    Edge

    Production from the Steelers’ running game has been down, and while Le’Veon Bell is a real threat, it hasn’t materialized much. Their standout center Maurkice Pouncey is banged up, but if he can go, he joins David DeCastro as two real stout pieces up front. The Cowboys will have to be ready for a significant challenge on the ground.

    When the Steelers pass the ball

    Edge

    When Ben Roethlisberger (knee) is right, there are few QBs in the league who are more difficult to defend and bring down. He can use his legs to stay alive on plays and can throw the ball as well as any player in the business. It will be a significant challenge for Byron Jones and JJ Wilcox as the Steelers love to focus on Antonio Brown underneath long enough to spring a speedster deep.

    Special teams

    Edge

    Dan Bailey gives the Cowboys a big advantage in the kicking game as the Steelers are rolling with Chris Boswell, who is having a very difficult season. Further, the Steelers had a punt blocked for a touchdown in Baltimore last week.

    Intangibles

    Edge

    One would imagine that Pittsburgh feels its season is on the line following three consecutive losses that have put everyone on edge in that proud city. The Cowboys roll in with a swagger and a spot on top of the NFC. They would love to keep the good vibes going.

    Bob Sturm’s spotlight

    For years, the best receiver in the NFL was Calvin Johnson, the amazing receiver from Detroit who was known simply as “Megatron.” He was given that name as a tribute to his absurd physical advantages that started with a frame of 6-5, 240 pounds. He used that along with speed and leaping ability to accumulate production that was off the charts.

    The Steelers’ Antonio Brown has put up dazzling numbers as well and has been the most productive receiver in the NFL since 2013. What may be surprising is that Brown does it at 5-10, 181 pounds. In 56 games, he has 430 receptions for 5,708 yards and 37 touchdowns, 700 more yards than Atlanta’s Julio Jones.

    Brown was taken with the 195th pick of the sixth round in the 2010 draft out of Central Michigan. He uses great speed, quickness and is a real technician to dominate on the perimeter. He wears No. 84 to show that “eight times four equals 32” for each team that passed on him in the draft.

    Not only is he unstoppable, but he is drastically underpaid at about $8 million a year in a league where elite WRs receive about twice that. There is no question that he will be a focus of the Cowboys’ banged-up secondary as he tries to become the first 100-yard receiver against Dallas this season.

    Sturm’s prediction: The Cowboys have the best road record in the NFL since 2014 at 15-5. It’s even more impressive when you consider that they are 14-0 on the road (regular season) in games started with either Tony Romo or Dak Prescott. In other words, their game travels well and the normal trappings of a road challenge don’t bother a team with this dominant offensive line. Can Prescott pass yet another difficult test? I think so, but it won’t be easy or decided before the fourth quarter. Cowboys, 30-28
