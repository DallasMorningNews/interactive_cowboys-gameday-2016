Cowboys Gameday: Week 2

Cowboys at Redskins

Noon, Sunday TV: Ch. 4

Radio: KRLD-FM (105.3), KMVK-FM (107.5, Spanish)

Line: Redskins by 3

    A win would mean ...

    The Cowboys avoid an 0-2 start to NFC East play and the season and get their first win behind rookie quarterback Dak Prescott, showing they can make it work while Tony Romo is hurt.

    A loss would mean ...

    The Cowboys fall to the Redskins in consecutive meetings for the first time since Washington swept Dallas during the 2012 season. Some hard questions could follow.

    Brandon George: Dak Prescott didn’t turn over the ball in his first NFL start last Sunday against the Giants, and the Cowboys still lost at home. This week brings a much different test for Prescott: He’ll make his first NFL road start, against the rival Redskins. Sure, Prescott has played in front of louder crowds in the SEC than what he’ll see Sunday at Washington. But this is the NFL, which comes at you much faster. The Cowboys believe Prescott is their future. They’ll have a much better feel for that after Sunday. Winning your first road start as a quarterback is a tall task. Prescott will keep it close but falter late. Redskins, 30-27

    Kate Hairopoulos: As Jason Garrett helpfully reminded us last week, running the ball in the NFL is hard. But it’s not as hard against Washington as it is against other teams. This will be the week rookie running back Ezekiel Elliott has some fun and finds some room to run behind a prideful offensive line that didn’t look as powerful as expected in run blocking last week against the New York Giants. Backup RB Alfred Morris will get his chances, too, against the team that drafted him and then let him go to its rival in free agency. As always, the run helps set up the rest of the offense. Cowboys, 27-21

    Jon Machota: All eyes will be on the matchup between Dez Bryant and Josh Norman. Things got heated between the two star players last Thanksgiving. Norman won that battle, holding Bryant to one catch for 6 yards on five targets. But the two-time Pro Bowl receiver wasn’t close to 100 percent, dealing with foot, ankle and knee injuries. Both players are healthy heading into Sunday’s game. Most believe Norman will shadow Bryant throughout, but there’s no guarantee. In Washington’s season opener, Norman was rarely on All-Pro receiver Antonio Brown. Against the Redskins, the Cowboys make a point to get Bryant more involved than they did last week, and he finishes with five catches for 75 yards and a touchdown. Cowboys, 24-23

    David Moore: One team returns a key player who was down with pneumonia for a few days. The other team’s most vocal player decided to release all of his medical records on Dr. Oz. Oh wait, wrong contest. This is supposed to be about that other game that will be played outside of our nation’s capitol this weekend. The Dallas- Washington rivalry doesn’t generate the same hatred and intensity it once did. But both teams find themselves in a precarious spot this early in the season. A Cowboys loss would give them two division losses in two weeks and blunt the excitement Dak Prescott sparked during the preseason. A Washington loss would give the Redskins two home losses by mid-September. Cowboys, 23-17

    Tim Cowlishaw: A week later than expected, Cowboys running game gets untracked and Dan Bailey comes up big again to break into win column. Cowboys, 23-16

    Rick Gosselin: Neither team plays much defense, and the defending NFC East champs can’t afford to lose back-to-back home games to start the season. Redskins, 30-27

    Barry Horn: Ezekiel Elliott has his first 100-yard game, and Dez Bryant quadruples his receptions and holds on to a fade for touchdown. But the defense wilts on a late fourth-quarter drive. Redskins, 24-23

    Kevin Sherrington: Cowboys still figuring out who they are. Redskins, 27-20

    Bob Sturm’s scouting report

    SituationEdgeIn the know

    When the Cowboys run the ball

    Edge

    After being shown some real resistance against the Giants, the Cowboys have been challenged to get back in sync. Ezekiel Elliott appeared a bit overeager and needs to show his normal patience in allowing things to develop. Washington certainly did not limit the Steelers on the ground, allowing 5.5 yards a carry to DeAngelo Williams.

    When the Cowboys pass the ball

    Edge

    Here is where Dallas needs to respond with a big performance and a deliberate attempt to get the ball into the hands of Dez Bryant. Dak Prescott was careful with his throws, avoiding the middle of the field and coverage. But to win on the road, there must be times to challenge the defense. Look for defensive coordinator Joe Barry to try to bait the rookie QB with disguises in the pass rush and the seven-man coverages behind it. Ryan Kerrigan and Preston Smith bring plenty of heat.

    When the Redskins run the ball

    Edge

    Bill Callahan’s zone running scheme started well last year, but by the end of the season the production was 30th in the league in yards per rush. Then the Redskins allowed Alfred Morris to leave for low money. And that has left them with almost nobody noteworthy to carry the ball. The good news for Washington is that Dallas was run over by the Giants and will surely be challenged again.

    When the Redskins pass the ball

    Edge

    On paper, the Redskins seem to have an impressive group of receivers, including their first-round pick, TCU’s Josh Doctson. But until they demonstrate any sort of change, their passing attack still appears to be forcing the ball to TE Jordan Reed and throwing horizontal passes to small receivers on the edge, with the occasional shot to DeSean Jackson over the top. Kirk Cousins generally won’t beat you with remarkable throws.

    Special teams

    Edge

    Jamison Crowder is a dynamic and shifty return man on punts who also is a real threat in the slot on offense. Washington is not considered strong on special teams, but it improved in 2015 across the board. For the Cowboys, Dan Bailey was fantastic last week in his normal fashion and kicked the game-winner in Washington last December.

    Intangibles

    Edge

    Both teams are coming off losses in Week 1 at home. This season, Washington faces a first-place schedule that includes a trip to Arizona and a visit from Carolina. whereas the Cowboys host Tampa Bay and travel to San Francisco. Neither team can afford to start 0-2, but the panic in the capital city will be immense if the Redskins drop another home game right off the bat.

    Bob Sturm’s spotlight

    The object of great intrigue during the Redskins’ offseason was their acquisition of cornerback JOSH NORMAN after an unexpected series of events allowed them to sign him quickly to a five-year, $75 million contract. The contract is the largest at his position in history, with a virtual guarantee of $50 million that is normally reserved for only the best quarterbacks in the business.

    Norman is a fantastic story — a player who was marginalized in the draft process before being developed into a force in the Carolina secondary. The oddity is that because the Panthers play almost exclusively zone coverages, they do not wish to spend large sums on defensive backs. In other words, they feel that given the strength of their pass rush and zone scheme, their corners are replaceable.

    The Redskins actually did well in coverage last year, which has many wondering about this record investment in something that did not appear terribly broken (perhaps similar to the Cowboys’ RB philosophy last spring). Further, on Monday night, they did not ask Norman to “travel” from side to side with Pittsburgh’s Antonio Brown, but rather stay on the defensive left side. He clearly has confidence against Dez Bryant, going back to last Thanksgiving’s matchup, but now all eyes are watching to see if he is asked to follow Bryant.

    Sturm’s prediction: The road team has won the last five in this series, and the teams seem like an even match. That said, the Redskins must bounce back on short rest, and Dallas has a QB making his first road start. Redskins, 23-21
